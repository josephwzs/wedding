Primary 1

Students will smoothly transition into Primary school by building a robust foundation in core areas such as reading, writing, listening and speaking in English and basic concepts and problem- solving skills in Mathematics.

Primary 2

As students find their footing in primary school, they need to build upon their foundation anchored in P1, to become confident and effective learners. We recognise that students succeed at their own pace, and have calibrated our curriculum to cater to students of different abilities.

Primary 3

Primary 3 marks a key transition for our students, as they shoulder increased academic demands, including a new Science subject. They will be guided to solidify and expand upon the concepts and skills acquired in Lower Primary, and apply them towards higher-order tasks.

Primary 4

As they move to Upper Primary, students need to solidify their concepts learnt in Lower Primary, while taking on more advanced skills. They will internalise new topics and concepts via our detailed concept papers and summary notes, which are complemented with study aids like mind-maps and diagrams.

Primary 5

In Primary 5, students must cope with new test components, challenging problems, and content- intensive topics. With little time left till the Primary 6 PSLE, students cannot afford to fall behind. We will help students stay ahead, and give them an advantage in preparing for the PSLE.

Primary 6

This year, students will take the PSLE, which determines their future secondary school. We will prepare them to tackle it confidently and maximise their chances of achieving their dream results. We will also provide holistic resources beyond academics to support our students in this gruelling year.

Secondary 1

As students embark on a new phase of their education journey in Secondary School, they must adapt to a more challenging academic environment. There are now many more subjects, with content-intensive topics, regardless of whether they are in the IP or O-Level track.

Secondary 2

In Secondary 2, students will reinforce their academic foundation, tackle even more challenging topics and sharpen their analytical & problem-solving skills. At the end of the year, they will also choose which subjects they will study in Upper Secondary (depending on their school results in S2).

Secondary 3

The increased academic rigour in Upper Secondary is a main reason why many students struggle when they enter S3. They have to contend with the increased breadth, depth and scope of various subjects and have to elevate their linguistic skills (both written & spoken).

Secondary 4

Secondary 4 is a crucial academic milestone for al students (regardless of whether they are in the IP or O- Level track). The vast majority of students will face the O-Level Exam, while IP students will need to fortify their foundations for their pre-university education (be it the A-Level or IB Diploma).
